## Business Context & **Geographical Perimeter**

Edmond de Rothschild Asset Management (EdRAM) is the asset management arm of the Edmond de Rothschild Group — an independent, family-owned financial group with roots going back over 250 years. EdRAM manages a diversified range of 40+ funds across equities, fixed income, multi-asset, and alternative strategies, distributed across five core European markets:  **France, Germany, Switzerland, Italy, and Spain** .

At the start of each year, the asset management industry enters a critical strategic moment: every major player publishes its investment outlook for the year ahead. These publications lay out macroeconomic forecasts — growth, inflation, interest rates, currency trends — and translate them into investment positioning across asset classes and thematic strategies. For a firm like EdRAM, understanding how competitors read the macro environment is not optional — it is a core input into product strategy, client advisory, and commercial prioritization.

Raphaël Bellaiche, Head of Product Management at EdRAM, oversees the full fund range and leads the competitive and strategic intelligence function within the Executive Committee. He has brought this challenge to Albert School because it sits at the intersection of two things he cares about: rigorous financial analysis, and the creative use of data and AI tools that the next generation of finance professionals is uniquely positioned to deploy.

**Your work will be used.** The insights you produce will inform how EdRAM's Sales teams are briefed and how the fund range is positioned commercially in 2026. Treat this accordingly.

All analysis must cover the following five markets, which represent EdRAM's core European distribution footprint:

* 🇫🇷 France
* 🇩🇪 Germany
* 🇨🇭 Switzerland
* 🇮🇹 Italy
* 🇪🇸 Spain

Competitor asset managers to be analysed must be active in at least one of these markets and manage  **more than €10bn in AUM** .

## Phase 1 — Competitive Macro Intelligence

### Objective

Aggregate the 2026 investment outlooks of approximately 20 major European asset managers, extract their macroeconomic forecasts and investment positioning, and compare them systematically against EdRAM's own 2026 outlook.

### Step 1 — Competitor Mapping *(before any scraping)*

Before collecting any outlook data, you must first establish a rigorous, documented list of the asset managers you will analyse. This is not a formality — it is a strategic exercise in itself. A poorly scoped competitor list will undermine everything that follows.

Your mapping must cover asset managers that are:

* Active in at least one of the five target markets
* Managing more than €10bn in AUM
* Publishing a 2026 investment outlook in accessible form

As a starting point, consider the following firms  *(non-exhaustive)* : Natixis, DNCA, Amundi, BNP Paribas AM, Pictet, UBS AM, DWS, Generali Investments, Eurizon, Allianz GI, Lombard Odier, Candriam, AXA IM.

**Bonus territory.** Teams that identify relevant competitors beyond this baseline — through Bloomberg, industry databases, or independent research — will receive bonus points on the coverage criterion of Deliverable 1. The quality of your mapping signals the quality of your strategic thinking before a single data point is scraped.

Document your mapping with: firm name, AUM, markets covered, and the source or format of their 2026 outlook publication.

### Step 2 — What to Cover *(minimum baseline) *and to be Compared to EDRAM

For each competitor on your final list, capture at minimum their published views on:

* **Macroeconomic indicators:** US GDP growth, Eurozone GDP growth, Chinese GDP growth, global inflation, interest rate levels (Fed, ECB, SNB), EUR/USD and other key FX pairs, emerging markets growth
* **Asset class positioning:** equities (large cap vs. small cap, geographic bias), fixed income (investment grade vs. high yield, duration), currencies, commodities
* **Investment themes:** AI, healthcare, energy transition, geopolitical risk, other thematic calls

Teams that capture conviction levels, scenario probabilities, or contrarian calls will produce richer dashboards and stronger insights.

### Methodology Guidance

* Competitor outlooks are typically published as PDF slide decks or reports on asset managers' websites, via Bloomberg, or through financial media
* You are encouraged to explore data scraping, automated extraction, and AI-assisted summarization to handle the volume of material efficiently — this is part of the challenge
* All data must be traceable to its source

## **Phase 2 — Sales Prioritization by Country**

### **Objective**

Building directly on the macro scenarios and insights from Phase 1, identify which EdRAM funds are best positioned to benefit from dominant economic trends in each of the five target markets — and formulate concrete, prioritized recommendations for EdRAM's Sales teams by country.

### **What to Cover**

For each of the five countries:

* Synthesize the dominant macroeconomic narrative emerging from local or regionally dominant asset managers
* Map that narrative against the EdRAM fund range: which strategies are best aligned with the expected environment?
* Identify the most relevant client profiles (institutional, wholesale, private banking) and assess their fit with recommended funds
* Define clear KPIs to measure commercial potential of each recommendation (e.g. addressable AUM, competitive positioning vs. local peers, expected performance under the macro scenario, fee competitiveness). All KPIs must be computed on the basis of  **explicit, documented hypotheses** .

### **Deliverable**

**Deliverable 3 — Country-by-Country Sales Recommendations** *Due: Sunday, March 22nd at 11:59 PM*

A structured report providing, for each of the five countries: a macro scenario summary, a ranked selection of EdRAM funds to prioritize, a client targeting recommendation, and the KPIs that substantiate the commercial case. The format should be directly usable by a Sales manager preparing a client meeting or a country briefing.

*The detailed evaluation grid for this deliverable will be shared in the dedicated assignment guidelines later in the BDD.*
