# Proposed Country Peer Sets

These peer sets are the starting point for the local macro lens. They are not market-share proofs; they are the practical manager groups most likely to shape local sales conversations in each market based on home-market relevance, regional influence, and presence in the source pack.

## France

- EdRAM benchmark
- Amundi
- BNP Paribas AM
- Carmignac
- Candriam
- Oddo BHF AM
- Natixis-like logic is relevant conceptually, but not in the current local corpus

Working logic:
France should reflect local competition from large domestic platforms plus visible French-speaking active houses.

## Germany

- AllianzGI
- DWS
- Berenberg
- Oddo BHF AM
- BlackRock
- UBS

Working logic:
Germany should lean toward institutional, platform, and macro-discipline competitors rather than purely private-bank narratives.

## Switzerland

- UBS
- Pictet
- Lombard Odier
- EFG
- Mirabaud AM
- Pictet / Lombard / UBS style private-bank framing should matter heavily

Working logic:
Switzerland should emphasize wealth-management and private-banking product suitability.

## Italy

- Anima
- Generali AM
- Amundi
- BNP Paribas AM
- Carmignac

Working logic:
Italy should likely be more income, carry, and distributor-network sensitive than Germany or Switzerland.

## Spain

- Santander AM
- BlackRock
- Amundi
- JP Morgan AM
- BNP Paribas AM

Working logic:
Spain should reflect private banking, fund-of-funds, and bank-distribution dynamics more than domestic equity-national-champion logic.

## Use Rule

Peer sets should be used to frame the country narrative.

They should not automatically determine the final fund picks.
