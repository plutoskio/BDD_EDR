# Step 2 - Competitive 2026 Outlook Comparison vs EdRAM

This document completes **Step 2** from [business_case_brief.md](./business_case_brief.md): for each competitor in the final universe, it captures the published 2026 macro view, asset allocation stance, and key investment themes, and places them next to the EdRAM benchmark.

## Method and legend

- Scope: `EdRAM + 20 competitors` from the validated local source pack in `outlooks/`.
- Sources used: only the local files in `/Users/milo/Desktop/BDD_EDR/outlooks`.
- Extraction principle: explicit published statements were kept; missing numbers were **not** backfilled with assumptions.
- Coding:
  - `Q` = explicit quantitative forecast in the source
  - `Ql` = qualitative / directional published view only
  - `ND` = not clearly disclosed in the accessible source pack
  - `+ / 0 / -` = positive, neutral, cautious or negative positioning
- Source caveats:
  - `UBS` source is `UBS CIO GWM`, not a pure UBS AM publication.
  - `Berenberg` source is a `Q1 2026 Horizon` document, not a classic full-year house view.
  - `Pictet` and `Candriam` sources are `docx` files and are treated as `unpaginated`.

## EdRAM benchmark

- **Macro core**: late-cycle but not recessionary world; the U.S. remains resilient but fragile because employment is slowing and the AI build-out may be bubble-like; Europe can surprise on the upside through Germany and capital-markets integration; China remains investable through a more pragmatic, cheaper AI model.
- **Rates and FX**: Fed independence is a live risk after Powell; BCE may need to stay accommodative if euro appreciation hurts competitiveness; the dollar is structurally weaker than consensus expected and traded around `1.16-1.19` vs EUR in the source material.
- **Equities**: prudent / selective; underweight crowded U.S. AI mega-caps and prefer European small caps, under-owned quality, U.S. value and healthcare.
- **Fixed income**: favor spread products over pure duration; hybrids, emerging-market debt and carry strategies stand out; European HY is attractive when credit risk is preferred to duration risk.
- **Themes**: Big Data, resilience, healthcare, European sovereignty / defense, and selective China / EM exposure.
- **Primary source**: `EDRAM - Outlook 2026.pdf`, especially pp. `10-15`, `16-22`.
